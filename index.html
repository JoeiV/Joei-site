About Me

Hi! I'm Joei, a Washington DC transplant hailing from, well, many places actually. So you want my story...? As a child I loved being active outdoors, but was also obsessed with art in pretty much all forms (10yr old me once wrote a note to my adult self about how I better be working as an animator for Disney). I also spent a fair amount of energy obsessing about how I could get on a "Good Will Hunting" level of genius (...the struggle continues).

Jumping right ahead to the point that I made a pivotal shift in my life: Oct. 11, 2001. On that day I swore to defend my country from all enemies, foreign & domestic, via the United States Navy. Always believing that if you want change you have to make it yourself, I was not about to sit by and watch another September 11th happen. Not something I remotely imagined myself doing - a couple months prior I was a full-time college student & front desk attendant at a hotel/casino in Las Vegas. But in hindsight, I would do it all the same. It might sound typical, but I am legitimately honored to have served my country. In the years since my Navy days, I have continued to the fight for the greater good - consulting in a variety of roles, mostly involving analyzing data in one way or another.

So, why web development & design you ask? Because it's one of the few fields that allow me to really tap into both the analytical and artistic parts of my mind. And it offers endless possibilities - with implications far beyond even a logical and artistic realm. Not to mention the countless things I'm anxious to learn, with each new discovery sparking even more.

Current obsessions:
Web scrapers, the psychology of great UX, running an ultra-marathon, Gary Clark Jr's latest
